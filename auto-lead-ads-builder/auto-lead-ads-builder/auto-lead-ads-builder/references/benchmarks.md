## Speed-to-Lead Impact on Show Rate

| Response Time | Contact Rate | Show Rate |
|---|---|---|
| < 1 minute | ~78% contact | ~52% show |
| 1–5 minutes | ~62% contact | ~44% show |
| 5–15 minutes | ~38% contact | ~31% show |
| 15–60 minutes | ~21% contact | ~18% show |
| > 1 hour | ~9% contact | ~8% show |

*Source: Meta automotive guidance + LeadSavvy Pro field data + dataset observation*

The 5-minute SLA is the minimum viable threshold. Sub-1-minute response (SMS webhook → auto-text) achieves 52% show rates in Elite campaigns.

## Form Type Impact

| Form Type | Avg Lead Volume | Avg Lead Quality | Avg CPL |
|---|---|---|---|
| More Volume (no review step) | High | Lower intent | Lower |
| Higher Intent (review + OTP option) | -30 to 40% vs. More Volume | Higher intent | +20–40% |
| Higher Intent + Phone OTP | -50% volume vs. More Volume | Highest intent | +40–60% |

**Recommendation:** Higher Intent for events (quality matters more). More Volume for ongoing inventory campaigns where BDC call volume is high and qualification happens over the phone.

## Lookalike Audience Size vs. Performance

| Lookalike % | Audience Size | CPL | Lead Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1% | Smallest, most similar | Lowest CPL | Highest quality |
| 2–3% | Balanced | Mid | Good |
| 4–5% | Scale, less precise | Higher CPL | Lower |
| 6–10% | Broad reach | Much higher CPL | Volume play only |

**Best practice:** Start at 1–2% from CRM past buyers. Scale to 3% only when volume is insufficient. Never go above 5% for lead quality campaigns.
